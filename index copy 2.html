Xinru Qiu, PhD

Computational Biologist | Pharmaceutical Sciences × Generative AI

At the intersection of AI and Drug Discovery.
I am a bilingual scientist bridging the gap between Pharmaceutical Sciences (ADMET, PK/PD, Toxicology) and Generative AI (Foundation Models, Single-Cell Genomics). I don't just build models—I engineer computational platforms that understand the biological and clinical constraints of drug development.

Currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at UC Riverside (Godzik Lab), I apply large-scale pretrained models across single-cell, multi-omic, and protein modalities to solve translational problems in immunology, infectious disease, and therapeutic discovery. My work integrates pharmaceutical sciences with computational biology to design AI models that are biologically grounded, mechanistically interpretable, and directly relevant to therapeutic decision-making.

Core Competency: Integrating wet-lab logic (GLP/IND-enabling studies) with state-of-the-art AI (scGPT, Geneformer, Agentic Workflows) to accelerate target identification and de-risk clinical translation.

I obtained my PhD in Genetics, Genomics, and Bioinformatics from UC Riverside in 2023, advised by Professor Adam Godzik. Download my resume.

Platform & Methodology

Building an AI-Native Discovery Platform

My work focuses on constructing an integrated computational platform for modern drug discovery，using single-cell foundation models to simulate biological systems and predict clinical outcomes before wet-lab validation.

Strategic Pillars:

  • In Silico Perturbation Screening: Leveraging Foundation Models to predict cellular responses to perturbagens (small molecules, CRISPR, biologics), reducing the cost of high-throughput screening.
  • Clinical De-risking: Mining large-scale single-cell atlases for patient stratification and predictive biomarker discovery, addressing the "Valley of Death" in Phase II trials.
  • Lab-in-the-Loop Optimization: Architecting active learning workflows where AI models propose optimal experiments to maximize information gain per experimental cycle.
  • Mechanistic Interpretability: Extracting interpretable Mechanism of Action (MoA) insights from foundation model embeddings for regulatory submissions and scientific validation.
